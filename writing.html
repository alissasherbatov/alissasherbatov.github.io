STEM

The Scream Formula: Into the Hidden World of Roller Coasters (November 2020 - June 2022)

ISBN-13: 979-8218014414

The goal of The Scream Formula, published on June 2nd, 2022, is to educate people - ranging from those who don't know any physics to those with an extensive technical background - on roller coasters. With this book, I also wanted to showcase the breadth of fields that roller coaster studies touch, including math, robotics, biology, computer science and even anthropology.

In addition to writing and formatting the book itself, I designed and produced its cover. As a bonus feature, the book features a curated list of roller coaster-related books and studies for those who would like a higher-level introduction. You may purchase the book and view more information on it here.


Design Thinking (July 2021 - April 2022)

Since April 2021, I have been an ambassador for Princeton IgniteSTEM, a club that organizes STEM education events. One of my projects as an ambassador was to co-write Design Thinking, a book of exercises that can be used by elementary/middle school teachers to expose students to engineering design. The goal with this book was to be able to introduce the students to a lot of complex concepts, including personalized learning, fusion energy, and mixed reality, which are some of the sections that I wrote exercises for. While itâ€™s not at all expected that the students will understand all the nuances of each topic (though I would have loved to explain tokamak physics to a fifth grader), the intention was to get the gears in their brains turning, and give them experience with the design process as a whole.

This book is currently in the publishing process. In April 2022, I organized an event on Princeton's campus with the other ambassadors to serve as a demonstration of how the exercises would flow and function in a classroom setting. Hundreds of students from many different schools around New Jersey showed up.
